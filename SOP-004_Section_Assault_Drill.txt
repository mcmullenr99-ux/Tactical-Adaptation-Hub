E-SQUADRON STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-004 - SECTION ASSAULT DRILL
Version 1.2 | Classification: UNCLASSIFIED | Last Reviewed: March 2026

========================================================================

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This Standard Operating Procedure defines the tactics, techniques and
    procedures for a two-fireteam section conducting a deliberate attack
    on a fixed enemy position.

1.2 This SOP applies to all section-level assaults conducted by E-Squadron
    elements in combined arms and independent operations.

1.3 All ranks are expected to know and execute this SOP without prompting
    from higher command.

========================================================================

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 Section: Two fireteams (Alpha and Bravo) totalling 8-10 personnel under
    a Section Commander (SC) and 2IC.

2.2 Support-by-Fire (SBF): Designated fire position from which one element
    provides suppressing fire while the other manoeuvres onto the objective.

2.3 Breach Point: The designated entry point into the objective area.

2.4 ERV: Emergency Rendezvous - fallback point if communications are lost
    or the mission fails.

2.5 SITREP: Situation Report - standardised report format passed to higher.

2.6 MARCH Protocol: Massive haemorrhage, Airway, Respiration, Circulation,
    Hypothermia - standard casualty assessment sequence.

========================================================================

3. PRE-ASSAULT ADMINISTRATION

3.1 ORDERS GROUP
    a) SC delivers full orders using the standard SMEAC format.
    b) All element commanders confirm understanding and brief their teams.
    c) No questions outstanding before move to line of departure.

3.2 EQUIPMENT CHECKS
    a) Personal weapon checks - cleared, loaded, safety on.
    b) Magazines: minimum 3 full per rifleman, 4 per automatic weapon.
    c) Grenades: 2 fragmentation, 2 smoke per fireteam minimum.
    d) Communications: Primary and alternate nets tested and confirmed.
    e) Medical: Individual first field dressings confirmed on person.

3.3 REHEARSALS
    a) Dry run of breach and clearance at model if time permits.
    b) Actions on contact rehearsed by all elements.
    c) Casevac procedure and casualty collection point (CCP) confirmed.

========================================================================

4. ASSAULT SEQUENCE

4.1 APPROACH
    a) Section moves tactically to FUP using bounding overwatch.
    b) Noise and light discipline enforced throughout approach.
    c) SC confirms final coordinates and adjusts plan if required.

4.2 DEPLOYMENT FROM FUP
    a) Alpha Fireteam deploys left flank to flanking assault position.
    b) Bravo Fireteam moves to Support-by-Fire position with line of
       sight to the objective.
    c) Both elements confirm in position via radio before fire opens.

4.3 FIRE AND MANOEUVRE
    a) SC gives execute signal - two radio clicks or code word EXECUTE.
    b) Bravo opens fire immediately, suppressing the enemy position.
    c) Alpha initiates assault, advancing in short bounds.
    d) Alpha confirms breach point clear before entry.
    e) Bravo shifts fire to flanks on Alpha confirmation - code word FALCON.

4.4 CLEARANCE PROCEDURE
    a) Alpha clears room by room using standard two-man entry drill.
    b) Callout: CLEAR or NOT CLEAR after each room or position.
    c) No element moves past unsecured rooms or positions.
    d) Bravo collapses inward once Alpha confirms 75 percent cleared.
    e) Trigger discipline throughout - positive ID before engaging.

========================================================================

5. CONSOLIDATION

5.1 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS ON OBJECTIVE SECURED
    a) SC calls TANGO on radio - objective secure.
    b) All elements establish 360-degree security immediately.
    c) Casualties collected at CCP - medic moves immediately.
    d) Weapons and ammunition check by all element commanders.

5.2 REPORTING
    a) SITREP to higher within 3 minutes of objective secured.
    b) Format: callsign, location, status, enemy, casualties, next action.
    c) Intelligence finds documented - not moved until cleared by SC.

5.3 EXPLOITATION
    a) Search objective for intelligence per SC direction.
    b) Reinforce positions, redistribute ammunition.
    c) Prepare for counterattack. Await orders for next phase.

========================================================================

6. ACTIONS ON CONTACT (UNPLANNED)

6.1 CONTACT FRONT
    a) Lead element returns fire and calls CONTACT FRONT immediately.
    b) Remainder takes cover and awaits orders.
    c) SC orders fire and movement or break contact drill.

6.2 CONTACT FLANK
    a) Element in contact returns fire and calls direction.
    b) Opposite element bounds to envelop or SC orders break contact.

6.3 CASUALTIES
    a) Immediate first aid by buddy - MARCH protocol.
    b) Casualty dragged to cover before further treatment.
    c) SC informed immediately. Assault continues unless SC orders halt.

6.4 COMMUNICATIONS FAILURE
    a) Element continues mission on last known orders.
    b) Consolidate at ERV if mission fails or casualties exceed 50 percent.
    c) SITREP sent on alternate net or via runner.

========================================================================

7. COMMAND AND SIGNAL

7.1 Command: SC commands the section. 2IC assumes command if SC falls.
7.2 Primary Net: Section Command Net.
7.3 Alternate Net: Squadron Command Net.
7.4 Code Words:
    - EXECUTE       Commence assault
    - FALCON        Breach confirmed, shift fire
    - TANGO         Objective secured
    - RALLYING CRY  Break contact, move to ERV

========================================================================

8. REVIEW

This SOP is reviewed quarterly or following any significant operational
incident. Lessons learned submitted to Section Commander in writing
within 48 hours of the incident occurring.
